Indian Land Area Conversion Rules Kya Hain?

India mein zameen napne ka koi single national system nahi chalta. Sarkari kaagzaat mein Square Feet, Square Yard (Gaj), Acre aur Hectare jaise standard units chalte hain, lekin gaon aur purane records mein aaj bhi Bigha, Biswa, Katha, Dhur, Marla, Kanal aur Guntha jaise regional units use hote hain. Sabse badi baat — in regional units ki value har state mein alag hoti hai. Isliye sahi conversion ke liye pehle apna state chunna zaroori hai.

Yeh tool sabhi conversions ek base unit (Square Feet) ke through karta hai, taaki har unit ka aapas mein hisaab accurate rahe. Neeche kuch popular states ke rules diye gaye hain.

Uttar Pradesh (UP) mein Bigha aur Biswa ka Hisab

UP mein Bigha do tarah ka hota hai — Pucca aur Kaccha. Aam taur par:

  • 1 Bigha (Pucca) = lagbhag 27,225 sq ft = 3,025 Gaj
  • 1 Bigha (Kaccha) = lagbhag 9,075 sq ft (Pucca ka kareeb ek-tihai)
  • 1 Bigha = 20 Biswa, yaani 1 Biswa (Pucca) = lagbhag 1,361 sq ft

Bihar mein Bigha, Katha aur Dhur ki Value

Bihar mein zameen Bigha, Katha aur Dhur mein naapi jaati hai. Standard reference:

  • 1 Bigha = 20 Katha = lagbhag 27,225 sq ft
  • 1 Katha = 20 Dhur = lagbhag 1,361 sq ft
  • 1 Dhur = lagbhag 68 sq ft

Punjab aur Haryana mein Marla aur Kanal kaise nikalte hain?

Punjab aur Haryana mein Marla aur Kanal sabse common units hain, aur inka system kaafi standard hai:

  • 1 Marla = 272.25 sq ft
  • 1 Kanal = 20 Marla = 5,445 sq ft
  • 8 Kanal = 1 Acre (43,560 sq ft)
